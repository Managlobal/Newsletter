 Editorial 

Although the health situation continues to disrupt our lives, it is clear that the perspectives are getting brighter and that many projects are getting back on track. - Review of Work Packages 2 & 3

As a reminder, Work Package 2 (WP2) aims to analyze the business practices of selected firms, their leadership styles and the SME sector in five countries, namely Ghana, Cameroon, Senegal, Morocco and the UAE. Using an ethnographic approach, the study profiles and traces the backgrounds of business firms and their leaders. The aim of this contribution is to understand the causes of business success and failure in Africa and the Arab countries. 

Work Package 3 aims to produce a series of monographs of companies in the UAE, Morocco, Senegal, Ghana and Cameroon that contribute to the development of these countries because of their strong growth and their spillover effect on the rest of the economy. 

In the following two excerpts, Ababacar Dieng (UCAD) and Aminu Mamman (University of Manchester) talk about the progress of their work packages and their respective research.

 The ManaGlobal project explores local management practices in selected African and Arab countries. The aim is to explore how companies and subsidiaries become profitable and grow. It will highlight the differences between the norms and standards of management resulting from globalisation and the ways of doing business at the local level. The process of merging these norms and management styles is called glocalization or hybridization. ManaGlobal's objective is to contribute to research on glocal management through surveys conducted by a pioneering team of 16 African, Arab and European partners from 8 countries, including researchers, business leaders and institutional decision-makers. See www.managlobal.hypotheses.org/
